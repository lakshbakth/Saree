A masterpiece in deep crimson, where every thread carries two thousand years of weaving wisdom.

This Kanjivaram silk is woven on traditional pit looms using pure mulberry silk threads and real gold zari. The deep crimson body is contrasted by a wide gold zari border featuring the iconic temple motif — a pattern that has been woven in Kanchipuram for centuries. The pallu showcases an intricate peacock and lotus design, requiring over 40 hours of work by a single master weaver.

Care Instructions
🫧
Dry Clean Only Do not machine wash. Dry clean to preserve the silk and gold zari.
🌿
Storage Fold with muslin cloth. Store in a cool, dry place away from sunlight.
🌡️
Ironing Iron on the reverse side at medium heat. Avoid direct iron on zari.
💨
Airing Air the saree after every use before storing to prevent moisture.
The Craft · Provenance
From the Looms of Kanchipuram

This saree was woven by a third-generation weaving family in Kanchipuram who have been practicing the art for over 70 years. Kanjivaram silk is unique for its interlocking warp technique — the body and border are woven separately and then interlocked, giving the saree its characteristic weight and durability. A Kanjivaram is not just a garment; it is an heirloom passed from mother to daughter.
